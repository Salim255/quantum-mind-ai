Measurements in Different Directions

In quantum systems, the outcome of a measurement depends strongly on the direction in which it is made.

When a particle’s spin is first measured in one direction (for example, vertical), and then measured again in a different direction (for example, horizontal), the results are not predictable from the first measurement.

Even if the particle was fully determined in the first direction, the second measurement behaves randomly: half of the results tend to align one way, and half the opposite way.

This shows that different measurement directions are independent, and knowing the result in one direction does not determine the result in another.
