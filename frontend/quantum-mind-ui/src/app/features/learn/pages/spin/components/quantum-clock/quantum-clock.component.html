The Quantum Clock

The quantum clock is a thought experiment used to understand how quantum systems behave when we measure them.

Instead of directly observing the direction of a pointer, we can only ask specific yes/no questions, such as whether it points in a given direction.

Unlike a classical system, the response is not based on revealing a hidden full state. Each measurement only produces one of two possible outcomes: the direction asked about, or its exact opposite.

This shows a key idea in quantum physics: the result of a measurement depends on the question being asked, not on revealing a pre-existing classical value.
