Single‑Qubit Gates and a Simple Circuit

In quantum computing, gates are unitary operations applied to qubits. A common gate is the Hadamard gate, which maps basis states as:

$$ H|0\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} (|0\rangle + |1\rangle), \quad H|1\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} (|0\rangle - |1\rangle) $$
